Our Vision

The checkout experience in grocery stores hasn't changed really since our parent's time. Undoubtedly, the main reason for this is usually that such inventory management systems are often very complex, and intervening in them is mostly associated with many problems. Nevertheless, when customers in a retail store a ready to make a purchase, they don't want to spend time in a line to finish their payment. Not only this reduces customer satisfaction, it could even mean that some customers decide to leave before they pay for their goods. Thus, a supermarket has to find the right balance to supervise many customers to improve company revenue and still guarantee an optimal user experience for the consumer.

Our Goal

The goal of our project is to offer a solution where customers are able to scan desired products in a supermarket using the personal smartphone and pay instantly afterwards at the cashier. By that traditional retail stores can significantly reduce their queuing times, but it also allows their employees to focus on crucial parts in the shop. This ensures that multiple transactions are processed quickly and reduces waiting times at checkouts. This leads to an optimal customer experience and an increase of sales. Additionally, retailers have the opportunity to see the current customers in the shop and get a quick insight of the shopping behaviour of their customers.

Scanning tips
Your camera works best to scan barcodes with good lighting and the proper distance. Hold items 6” - 9” behind your camera with the barcode as flat & clearly visible as possible. You’ll notice it often times picks up the barcode even without the perfect distance or flatness, but it’s good practice.
Discount issues
The most common issue with discounts is not purchasing the required number of items for the deal. Please check that first. If you need assistance with a discount, alert the clerk when exiting thru the Skip Lane. The clerk can adjust the price once confirmed. Tip: Snap a pic of the shelf tag/discount sign to speed up the price check. *See the Coupons section of Support for more detail regarding coupons.
Price checks
Prices are updated each morning, and throughout the day, and should display the most up-to-date price in the store’s system. If you would like a price check, the clerk in the Skip Lane can adjust the price once confirmed. Tip: Snap a pic of the shelf tag to speed up the price check.
How to check out
Once you’re ready to go, tap the Pay button at the bottom of the screen in the cart and head to the skenn. Line for a clerk to review your receipt. The skenn. Lane is typically next to the self checkout kiosks or at the Guest Services area.
